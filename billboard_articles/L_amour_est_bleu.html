"L'amour est bleu" (French pronunciation: [lamu Å …õ bl√∏]; "Love Is Blue") is a song recorded by Greek singer Vicky Leandros with music composed by Andr√© Popp and French lyrics written by Pierre Cour. It represented Luxembourg in the Eurovision Song Contest 1967 held in Vienna, placing fourth.

It has since been recorded by many other musicians, most notably French orchestra leader Paul Mauriat, whose familiar instrumental version ‚Äìrecorded in late 1967‚Äì became the first number-one hit by a French lead artist to top the Billboard Hot 100 in the United States.

Background

Conception

[edit]

"L'amour est bleu" was composed by Andr√© Popp with French lyrics by Pierre Cour. It describes the pleasure and pain of love in terms of colours (blue and grey) and elements (water and wind). The lyrics of the English version ("Blue, blue, my world is blue ‚Ä¶") focus on colours only (blue, grey, red, green, and black), using them to describe components of lost love.[1]

The Compagnie Luxembourgeoise de T√©l√©diffusion (CLT) internally selected "L'amour est bleu" as its entry for the 12th edition of the Eurovision Song Contest, and a Greek-born 17-year-old Vicky Leandros as its performer as Vicky.[2]

In addition to the French-language original version, she recorded the song in English ‚Äìas "Colours of Love" with lyrics by Bryan Blackburn‚Äì,[3] German ‚Äìas "Blau wie das Meer" with lyrics by Klaus Munro‚Äì, Italian ‚Äìas "L'amore √® blu"‚Äì, and Dutch ‚Äìas "Liefde is zacht"‚Äì,[1] that were release in nineteen countries.[4][5]

On 8 April 1967, the Eurovision Song Contest was held at the Gro√üer Festsaal der Wiener Hofburg in Vienna hosted by √ñsterreichischer Rundfunk (ORF), and broadcast live throughout the continent. Vicky performed "L'amour est bleu" as the second song of the evening. Claude Denjean conducted the event's live orchestra in the performance of the entry.[6]

Aftermath

[edit]

Vicky Leandros went on to win Eurovision five years later with the song "Apr√®s toi", again representing Luxembourg.

"L'amour est bleu" achieved greater success through cover versions of the song by other artists. Some forty years after its original release, "L'amour est bleu", along with Domenico Modugno's "Nel blu dipinto di blu" (better known as "Volare") and Mocedades' "Eres t√∫", still counts as one of very few non-winning Eurovision entries ever to become a worldwide hit. According to Paul Mauriat, who conducted/recorded an orchestral "easy listening" version of "Love Is Blue", he chose the song because it was published by his label, Philips Records even though he was not fond of the song. A DJ in Minneapolis played the recording and asked the audience to respond, and was inundated with phone calls about the song, and interest in the song then quickly spread around the country.[18]

The song became a number-one hit in the USA for five weeks in February and March 1968, the first recording by a French artist to top the Billboard Hot 100. (It remained the only French song to top the chart until 2017, when Daft Punk was a featured artist on Canadian artist The Weeknd's number-one hit "Starboy".) Mauriat's version became a gold record, and its five-week run at the top is the second longest of any instrumental of the Hot 100 era, after "Theme from A Summer Place". The song also spent 11 weeks atop Billboard's Easy Listening survey, and held the longest-lasting title honours on this chart for 25 years. Billboard ranked the record as the No. 2 song for 1968.[19] It is the best-known version of the song in the United States. The Mauriat recording also reached No. 2 in Canada[20] (No. 12 Year End),[21] and No. 12 on the UK Singles Chart.[22] The Mauriat album containing "Love Is Blue", Blooming Hits, also reached No. 1 on the Billboard Top LP's and Tapes chart for five weeks. The song sold fewer than 30,000 units in France, but 2 million singles and 800,000 LPs were sold in the US.[18]

In popular culture

[edit]
  • Mauriat's version was featured repeatedly in an episode of Chris Carter's television series Millennium titled "A Room with No View", which originally aired on 24 April 1998 on the Fox Network. During the episode, the omnipresent melody is used by a kidnapper to brainwash a group of youths.[23]
  • His version is also briefly heard in The Simpsons episodes "There's No Disgrace Like Home" and "The Blue and the Gray."
  • It was played over the closing credits of Mad Men's sixth-season episode "The Flood", set in April 1968.[24]
  • The harpsichord riff from Mauriat's version was also sampled by the English electronica duo J Walk in their song "French Letter", as part of their 2002 album A Night on the Rocks.
  • Mauriat's version is used as the theme music for American music producer Rick Rubin's podcast Tetragrammaton.

Other covers

[edit]
  • Three other recordings of the song charted on Billboard Hot 100 together with Paul Mauriat's version at the same time in 1968. Al Martino's "Love is Blue" peaked at No. 57 on the Billboard Hot 100 and No. 3 on the Billboard Adult Contemporary charts, and was the title song of one of his 1968 albums.[41] Claudine Longet's "Love Is Blue (L'amour est bleu)" peaked at No. 71,[42] while Manny Kellem's version reached No. 100.[5]
  • Jeff Beck recorded a rock interpretation of Mauriat's version in 1968. It reached No. 20 in Ireland,[29] and No. 23 on the UK Singles Chart.[43]
  • The Dells recorded a soul medley, "I Can Sing a Rainbow/Love Is Blue", which reached No. 22 in the Billboard Hot 100 and No. 5 in the Billboard R&B Singles charts in the US, No. 10 in the Dutch chart,[44] No. 18 in Ireland,[29] and No. 16 in the UK charts, in 1969.[45][46]
